1 Review Questions

a. Inequality of income across countries was already very wide at the end of the medieval period.

  • Answer

    False: It started widening after the industrial revolution. This is due to the fact that before the revolution growth was almost null around the world. The production was very close to subsistence level and there were no investments. The technological progress of the revolution was the catalyst of growth which developed differently and caused inequalities among different countries.
